What is a Credit Report?

When finance companies carry out background checks on a person who has applied to them to borrow money they will receive a Credit Report. This is a detailed analysis of relevant information on the prospective borrower, such as current income and their previous borrowing history. What it reveals will have a significant impact on how much the company will be prepared to lend and under what terms.

There are several specialist agencies that will prepare a Credit Report on behalf of a lender. The report will show a range of relevant information on the prospective customer, such as whether they own their own home, are employed and how much they earn. It will list any existing loans, cards or instalment plans currently being managed by the customer. It will also reveal any previous borrowing in recent years and how well it was managed.

A Credit Report may also contain a rating or score based upon the customer’s borrowing history. This rating is calculated according to how much a person is currently borrowing, in what ways, how much they have borrowed in the past and how often they have missed payments. A high rating is better than a low one. A low score of, say, 400 will suggest the customer is a risky proposition but a rating of 800 will suggest they are used to borrowing money and have always made their payments promptly and in full.

A good Credit Report is crucial to the decision to lend the applicant money and how much, over what period of time and at what interest rates. A customer with a low or middling score may be seen as a bad risk and the lender will only consider lending them a comparatively small amount of money. They may well load the interest rate so that it is much higher than it would be for a customer with a better score. This does not just apply to people whose report shows a poor financial history, it will also affect those who have little or no history of borrowing money, such as young adults.

It’s a good idea to check your own rating

You can apply to see your own Credit Report at little cost. By doing so you will be able to ensure that the information presented on the report is accurate. Mistakes do occasionally occur and it has been known for a poor rating to be given just because a line of address on the electoral record was slightly misspelled or missing. Checking your own report can also help expose identity theft.

The best time to order a Credit Report is just before applying for a big loan, such as a mortgage. This way you will be able to forestall any problems due to inaccuracies. Every time a loan is rejected another negative number is added to the rating, so it is better to find out about problems before rather than after an application to borrow money. Your request for a Credit Report will show up on the report itself. This won’t affect your rating but if you apply too many times, this may look suspicious, so don’t overdo it.
