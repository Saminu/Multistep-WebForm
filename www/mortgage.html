What are Long term Loans?

Long term Loans are one of the most popular ways to obtain credit. The money borrowed is paid back over a fixed number of regular payments. Until recently they have been the traditional, long-term credit solution but Long term Loans are now taking their place among bad credit and short-term solutions.

Long term Loans are flexible and affordable

The Long term Loans model has been around for many years. It allows the customer to borrow a sum of money ranging from £500 up to tens or even hundreds of thousands of pounds and to pay it back over an agreed number of months or years. A mortgage on a house is a typical example. The repayments are made in manageable amounts at regular intervals, usually once a month.

Interest will of course be added to the sum borrowed but this too is spread across the repayments. Some lenders will weigh the repayments so that the interest is paid off first before the rest of the borrowed money.

Long term Loans allow the customer to manage their repayments effectively. They provide great flexibility. The money can be borrowed for as short a period as six months to – as in the case of a mortgage, for example – several decades. Generally, the longer the term, the smaller each monthly repayment will be. This means the borrower can ensure the repayments will remain comfortably within their budget. By determining how much they are able to afford month on month, they can arrange terms with the lender that will suit them best.

Now available for crisis solutions

Long term Loans have tended to remain the preserve of long-term borrowing but with an increasing range of credit solutions being made available, particularly online, they have now entered the short-term arena.

Short-term solutions were created for people who have experienced an unexpected crisis and need to borrow money quickly. It might be that they have been suddenly taken ill and will be unable to earn money for a few weeks or their car may have broken down and they simply don’t have the money to fix it. The money is needed immediately or the financial crisis will only get worse. These schemes also prove attractive to people with a poor credit rating who have found it hard to borrow money elsewhere.

These quick, short-term solutions are often referred to as ‘payday’ schemes because they are used to bridge a shortfall between one salary payment and the next. They are usually paid off in full, plus interest, in just one repayment and usually within one month. However, customers often find it hard to make the repayment because they have not had time to counteract the effect of the unexpected cash shortfall. They end up borrowing again – and again.

Payday solutions have recently been made available by some lenders in the form of Long term Loans, so that they can be paid off over several months, giving the customer time to get back on track with their finances and manage their repayments more effectively. The high interest rates charged on such solutions remain the same, however.
